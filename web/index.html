4. Ethics & Socioeconomic Bias in ML

Unveiling historical racial inequalities embedded in public baseline datasets.

Redlining and the Demise of the Boston 'B' Variable

In their landmark 1978 paper, Harrison & Rubinfeld included a demographic variable designated as B, defined mathematically as:

B = 1000 • (Bk - 0.63)2

Where Bk represents the proportion of Black residents in a given neighborhood block. This metric heavily penalized communities with moderate integration, mathematical modeling that directly quantified and justified racial redlining policies and systemic mortgage disparities of the era.

Our Ethical Stance: While we successfully reproduced this historic model to demonstrate the mathematical mechanics of multivariate Ordinary Least Squares (OLS), we explicitly acknowledge this bias. In modern production environments, engineers should seek cleaner demographic-free frameworks like the Ames Housing Dataset to eliminate the propagation of structural historic inequities.

5. Mathematics & Formulas

Revisiting the optimization landscape of linear models from Andrew Ng's Machine Learning Specialization.

Mean Squared Error Cost

J(w, b) = 12m ∑ ( fw,b(x(i)) - y(i) )2

Calculates the mean squared error variance across all training instances. In our vectorized engine, this operates as a dot-product transposal for computational acceleration.

Vectorized Parameter Gradient

∂J/∂w = 1m XT (Xw + b1 - y)

Computes the exact derivative slope of the error surface. Passing the transposed matrix enables updating all feature weights simultaneously without iterative scalar looping.

Gradient Descent Updates

w ← w - α • ∂J/∂w

Steps features weights against the direction of error slope. Tuning the learning rate (α) controls the step convergence duration without causing weight oscillation.
